# Feature Specification: Music Stem Separation

**Feature Branch**: `003-stem-separation`
**Created**: 2026-03-22
**Status**: Placeholder
**Input**: Separate a mixed audio track into isolated stems (drums, bass, vocals, other instruments) and generate per-stem timing tracks. Support vocal isolation down to primary vs. secondary vocal layers.

---

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 - Generate Stem-Based Timing Tracks (Priority: P1)

A user runs analysis on an MP3 and receives timing tracks derived from isolated stems
rather than the mixed audio. Each stem (drums, bass, vocals, other) produces its own
timing track, giving cleaner and more element-specific marks than HPSS-based separation.

**Why this priority**: HPSS produces crude approximations. True stem separation removes
bleed between instruments, dramatically improving timing mark accuracy for each element.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** an MP3 file, **When** the user runs analysis with stem separation enabled,
   **Then** the output includes at least four named stem tracks: drums, bass, vocals, other.
2. **Given** stem-separated tracks alongside existing HPSS tracks, **When** the user
   reviews quality scores, **Then** stem-derived tracks score consistently higher than
   HPSS-derived equivalents for the same musical element.
3. **Given** a machine without a GPU, **When** stem separation runs, **Then** it
   completes using CPU and the user is informed of expected processing time.
4. **Given** stem separation is not installed, **When** analysis runs, **Then** the
   pipeline completes without stem tracks and notifies the user that they are unavailable.

---

### User Story 2 - Primary and Secondary Vocal Separation (Priority: P2)

For songs with multiple vocal layers (lead vocal + backing vocals, harmony parts, call
and response), the user can obtain separate timing tracks for the dominant vocal and
secondary vocal content, enabling independent choreography of vocal-responsive lighting.

**Why this priority**: Vocal lighting is one of the most expressive elements. Blending
lead and backing vocals into a single track loses the distinction that makes vocal
choreography interesting.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** an MP3 with a clearly dominant lead vocal, **When** vocal sub-separation
   is requested, **Then** the output includes a primary vocal timing track and a
   secondary vocal timing track with meaningfully different mark distributions.
2. **Given** an instrumental track with no vocals, **When** vocal separation runs,
   **Then** the tool indicates no vocal content was detected and produces no vocal tracks.
3. **Given** a song where backing vocals are minimal, **When** the secondary vocal
   track is reviewed, **Then** it contains fewer marks than the primary vocal track.

---

### Edge Cases

- Songs with heavily processed or pitch-shifted vocals (auto-tune, vocoder).
- Songs where "vocals" are primarily rap/spoken word rather than melodic singing.
- Very short files or files with sparse stem content (e.g., solo piano).
- Stem separation on tracks that are already stems (e.g., a drum-only file).

---

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

- **FR-001**: The tool MUST support stem separation as an optional analysis step that
  produces named timing tracks per isolated stem.
- **FR-002**: Standard stems MUST include: drums, bass, vocals, and other instruments.
- **FR-003**: Vocal stems MUST support sub-separation into primary and secondary vocal
  tracks where distinct vocal layers are detectable.
- **FR-004**: Stem-derived timing tracks MUST integrate into the existing scoring and
  selection workflow alongside all other algorithm outputs.
- **FR-005**: Stem separation MUST be an optional dependency — the pipeline MUST
  complete without it, skipping stem tracks and warning the user.
- **FR-006**: The user MUST be able to enable or disable stem separation via configuration
  or CLI flag without modifying source code.
- **FR-007**: Stem separation MUST use the best available open-source model at the time
  of implementation; the choice MUST be documented with benchmarks.

### Key Entities

- **StemSet**: The collection of separated audio stems produced from a single MP3 — each
  stem is a named waveform (drums, bass, vocals, other).
- **VocalLayers**: Sub-separation of the vocals stem into primary and secondary layers.
- **StemTimingTrack**: A TimingTrack generated by running onset/peak detection against
  an isolated stem rather than the mixed audio.

---

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

- Stem-derived drum timing tracks contain at least 20% fewer false positive marks than
  HPSS-derived drum tracks on a reference test set.
- Users can produce stem timing tracks from a 3-minute song in under 60 seconds on
  Apple Silicon hardware.
- Primary vocal tracks and secondary vocal tracks are distinguishable (mark distributions
  differ by at least 30%) on songs with known lead + backing vocal arrangements.
- Stem separation failure (unavailable dependency) does not prevent any other timing
  tracks from being generated.

---

## Assumptions

- Research already completed; Demucs `htdemucs` is the selected primary model (see `research.md`).
- Primary/secondary vocal separation may require a second-pass model or heuristic on top
  of the Demucs vocal stem — the exact approach is TBD during planning.
- "Best available" vocal sub-separation may initially be a heuristic (energy envelope
  analysis) rather than a learned model.

---

## Out of Scope

- Stem export as audio files (WAV/MP3 output of isolated stems is not a goal).
- Real-time or streaming stem separation.
- Per-instrument separation beyond the standard 4-stem set (guitar, piano as separate
  stems may be explored but are not required).
